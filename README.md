## What This Is

This is a draft chapter for an AI systems textbook. The chapter is about workflow architecture in high-stakes agentic AI systems — specifically, why the structure of how an agent processes information matters more than how smart the agent is.

---

## What's Included

- **Chapter 6 draft** — full prose chapter with opening case, core mechanism, failure analysis, and student problems
- **Author's Note** — explains design choices, tool use, and honest self-assessment of what works and what doesn't
- **Demo Code** — Demo code demonstrating the primitive building blocks for workflow design
- **Video Recording** — Full video recording explaining the chapter content and demonstrating the demo code

---

## What the Chapter Covers

Three workflow building blocks — sequential processing, parallel execution, and suspend/resume gating — and how choosing the wrong one in the wrong place produces systems that are either too slow to be useful or too fast to be safe. The chapter uses a clinical documentation agent as its central case.

---

## A Few Things to Know Before Using This

- The Oracle Health framing is based on public product principles, not internal documentation. The architectural details are illustrative.
- The opening overdose scenario and the premature finalization case are hypotheticals. Both are clearly labeled as such in the text. The failure classes they represent are real.
- The learning outcomes at the top are written to Bloom's Taxonomy and map directly to specific sections of the chapter.

Students who have already read the earlier chapters in this book — particularly the reasoning architecture chapter and the memory chapter. This chapter assumes familiarity with what an agentic system is. It does not re-explain that.
